8. Paella - Spain

Paella, in Spanish cuisine, a dish of saffron-flavoured rice cooked with meats, seafood, and vegetables. Originating in the rice-growing areas on Spain's Mediterranean coast, the dish is especially associated with the region of Valencia.

It is a dish with rice as the main ingredient. It also contains chicken, vegetables, and seafood. All these ingredients are served in a pan. The dish tastes salty, and it does not contain onion. It is a dry dish, and all the liquid used gets absorbed by its ingredients. Originally, paella made in Valencia was a lunchtime meal for farmers and farm laborers. Workers would gather what was available to them around the rice fields. This often included tomatoes, onions, and snails. Rabbit or duck was a common addition, or chicken less often.
